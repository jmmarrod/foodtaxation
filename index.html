About the Network

Our Mission & Objectives

The Food Taxation Network is an international research association dedicated to studying the impact and potential of food taxation. Our mission is to create a global hub for researchers and institutions interested in how fiscal policy can improve health and environmental outcomes, while analyzing the associated economic and social consequences.

Our primary objectives include:

  • To facilitate the exchange of experiences and knowledge among researchers from different countries.
  • To combine efforts in joint research and collaborative projects.
  • To create an annual or biennial meeting forum to present findings and discuss policies.
  • To establish a network with at least one expert per country to ensure a global perspective.

Scope of Study

Our collaborative research will focus on generating evidence-based insights across these critical domains:

  • Policy Design & Implementation: Analyzing the effectiveness of different tax mechanisms, including excise taxes on specific products (e.g., sugar-sweetened beverages) versus broader value-added tax (VAT) differentiations to influence consumption patterns.
  • Impact Evaluation: Developing robust methodologies to measure the real-world effects of food taxes on consumption, health outcomes, environmental metrics, and economic indicators across diverse populations.
  • Consumer Behavior & Industry Response: Investigating how consumers and food industries adapt to new tax policies, including product reformulation, marketing strategies, and shifts in purchasing habits.
  • Global Knowledge Exchange: Creating a platform for sharing case studies, best practices, and research findings from different countries to accelerate learning and inform effective policymaking globally.

Connection to the SDGs

The network's work is intrinsically linked to the United Nations' Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). By promoting fiscal policies that encourage healthy diets and sustainable food systems, we directly contribute to goals such as Good Health and Well-being (SDG 3), Responsible Consumption and Production (SDG 12), Climate Action (SDG 13), and Reduced Inequalities (SDG 10).

Global Scope

Given that we expect the scope to be global, or at least European, all network documentation and communications will be in English to ensure maximum international collaboration and dissemination.
